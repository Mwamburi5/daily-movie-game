# Marquee · Card Art Prompt (Fronts: A + B)

Paste the block below into Google Stitch or Claude Design. It asks for **both**
fronts side by side: **A in screen-print**, **B in vector soft-3D**. Texture is the
headline requirement for each. Swap the example movie if you want a different test
card; Titanic is used so both renders are directly comparable.

---

## COPY-PASTE PROMPT

```
Design TWO mobile game card FRONTS, side by side, for a movie-connection card game
called "Marquee." Both cards are for the same film so I can compare art styles:
TITANIC (1997). Portrait tiles, roughly a 3:4 ratio, with soft die-cut corners
(about a 5mm radius) and a clean border that separates the card from a dark table.

The two cards must use DIFFERENT textures, and texture is the most important part of
this brief. Render each one so the surface finish is unmistakable even in a thumbnail.

CARD 1 — "REPERTORY TICKET", SCREEN-PRINT TEXTURE.
A vintage cinema admission-ticket aesthetic. The whole card must look like a real
silkscreen print: a limited ink palette (3 to 4 colors), visible halftone dot grain,
slight ink misregistration where colors overlap, matte uncoated paper stock with a
faint paper tooth, and chunky flat shapes. Warm cream paper base with one deep ink
accent. Add a perforated ticket stub edge down the left side. Bold slab-serif title.

CARD 2 — "MARQUEE LIGHTS", VECTOR SOFT-3D TEXTURE.
A modern, premium theater-marquee aesthetic. The whole card must look like polished
soft-3D vector art: smooth gradients, gentle volumetric lighting, soft contact
shadows, subtly glossy rounded forms, clean anti-aliased edges, no grain. A dark
theatrical field framed by a row of glowing marquee bulbs and slim art-deco gold
trim. The title sits in lit, dimensional lettering as the hero element.

KEY ART (same symbolic subject on both, styled to match each card's texture):
An ORIGINAL, symbolic illustration that evokes Titanic WITHOUT copying any real movie
poster, film still, studio logo, custom title font, or actor likeness. Use setting and
mood only: a grand four-funnel ocean liner at an angle on a moonlit ocean, dramatic
dusk-to-night sky, glowing portholes, calm water. No people, no faces, no real logos.

SHARED LAYOUT RULES (apply to BOTH cards):
- TOP-LEFT INDEX: hard in the top-left corner, place a small GENRE PIP that is double-
  coded as BOTH a color AND a shape (Titanic = Drama = teal circle), immediately
  followed by a LARGE, highly legible title "TITANIC". Title must be readable when the
  card is overlapped in a fanned hand. Put the year "1997" in the top-right.
- HERO ART sits in a framed window in the upper-middle, with an inner border.
- CONNECT-CREDITS appear as a short scored list below the art, each row showing a role
  glyph, a name, and a point value: Leonardo DiCaprio +1, Kate Winslet +1,
  James Cameron (director) +2. Role glyphs are also double-coded: actor = blue circle,
  director = amber square.
- A small teal "DEEP CUT" badge sits on the art.
- Keep the center of the card clean enough that the art reads at small size.
- Use bold, universal iconography for any special markers, never tiny text.

OUTPUT: the two fronts side by side at high resolution, each clearly labeled
underneath ("A · Repertory Ticket · screen-print" and "B · Marquee Lights · vector
soft-3D"). Fronts only. Make them look like a cool, collectible, movie-obsessed card
game.
```
